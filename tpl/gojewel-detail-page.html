Design Challenge

How might we improve the experience of buying jewelries online using technology?

Solution

GoJewel is technology platform which connects customer and jewelery seller. You can rent a jewelry using the app for as long as you want and return it whenever you like. GoJewel will also help you find a jewelry matching your needs and you can try it using augmented reality before even renting it.

Contribution

Research Analysis, Ideation and story boarding, Wireframes, Proto.io prototype, Production and post production.

Key Takeaways
  • Story telling is a fundamental part of design process. Stories help you identify needs.
  • Understanding the concept of need and solutions. Needs are permanent, solutions are temporary. Building an experience around user need helps in making important design decisions, identify limitations and building effective strategies.
  • While designing interfaces, I learned how user perceive what they expect. How designers can leverage user’s recognition capability over recalling.
  • Experience economy: Difference between commodities, goods, services and experience. Why designers should strive for experience and aim for transformation.
  • Learned different techniques of visual and information design, prototyping and user testing.
  • Story telling using video is an effective tool to test your idea and convey message to users. I learned different techniques of storyboarding, direction and production.

Research



Needfinding

Storytelling is one of the integral parts of needfinding. To find the need of people we start paying attention to small things and start getting deeper in conversations with people in our daily life. After collecting different stories individually, team shared stories with each other and decided to proceed with story which represent the actual need of the people.


Stories

Carol is a female, fashion editor, and 25 years old. She plans to attend a dinner party two days later. She started to prepare for the party by buying some clothes and jewelry. So she walks into a jewelry shop and one necklace caught her attention. However she doesn’t want to wear the same jewelry at different parties. She might wear that jewelry for one or two times after buying it and the price do not seems worth the value.

Sarah is a shop assistant. She found most of her customer inclined towards renting the jewelry instead of buying it. To make it available for bigger audience and increase her earning from luxury jewelry, she started to think about another ways to sell or rent her products. Most of the times her luxury items spends a lot of time in showcases before someone buy it.



Contextual Inquiry

We conducted contextual research to understand in what ways and in what context people wear jewelries and what systems they use as for now to achieve their goal. We developed a user journey on the basis of data we collected and identified pain points in current user journey.



Primary Persona

Name: Jemila
Age: 25
Job: Fashion Editor

Jemila usually get a lot of invitations from fashion designers. Since she has strong ties with fashion industry people look at her for setting trends, that being said she wants to wear a different jewelry and dresses every time she join any event. She enjoys her life and is capable of buying luxurious jewelries. But after wearing couple of times she tends to loose interest and want something different and new and this makes her collection useless and waste of money.


Secondary Persona

Name: Lisha
Age: 26
Job: Fashion Designer

Lisha runs a independent jewelry design company. Jemila and Lisha have similar life styles but different working area. As a fashion designer she always tries to bring something new and innovative in market which means she deals with luxurious jewelry most of the time. And these expensive jewelries are really hard to sell. This sometimes discourages her to do innovation and set restrictions on her work and choice of element.



Competitive Research

Progression towards Experience

Using Basis of The Experience Economy we start thinking how to take this product from "Product" to a service and then eventually an Experience. We identified several needs and services which can stage our product as service and then experience.

  • Provide to-the-point search mechanism to help users find their desired jewelry in as little time as possible.
  • Provide suggestions on the basis of dress color and event type.
  • Help users in trying jewelry on their smart phones using Augmented Reality to help them decide if the product is right fit for them.

Storytelling through Video

There are a lot of different methods to test your idea. And storytelling video is one of them. We kicked off this project using stories from people and we tested this idea through story telling. Using the video, we conveyed the idea and observe reactions from our audience.
Our personas performed the particular scenario which represents the need to wear a jewelry. And the audience watching this video actually perceives the whole idea and the need of the people. We omitted all the unnecessary details from story and shoot a video using as limited details as possible to observe the validity and test if solution is actually addressing and fulfilling the need.

Scenario

SCENE 1
In the jewelry shop, Jemlia wants to buy jewelry in the store. So she walks in the store and looking for different kinds of jewelries. She found a beautiful jewelry. She picked it up and walks towards the mirror and tried it with a big smile on her face. But when she decided to buy it and saw the price tag, she was surprised by the price. So Anne had to put it back and leave the store.

SCENE 2
Jemila was having a lunch with her friend Lisah. Lisah was wearing a trendy and shiny jewelry. Jemila was surprised that her friend is wearing an expensive jewelry which she already saw in the jewelry store. Lisah saw the shocked face on Jemila and told her about GO JEWELRY app which she used. From this app Jemila can see that the original price is $5800, but rent per day is only $100. Jemila was excited and download the app. She started using this app to rent the jewelry for herself. She places an order with a gentle smile on her face.
