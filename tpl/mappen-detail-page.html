Map + Happening! Mappen. This app tells you about different experiences people had on specific places. Users can share their experience either good, average or bad and pin it to the map. The cluster of similar experiences forms a heat map on the area map. This helps user being informed about what’s happening around you. People can search and get notified about what are people’s experiences and how they can improve theirs.

Participation: UX Designer, Researcher

Initial Prompt

How might we educate people about laws and regulations to help them make better choices in future?

Design Process

The overall process was non-linear and some stages was extensively iterative. We followed Ideo design thinking process as case studies.

Quantitative | Surveys

Before conducting interviews and formulating insights we decided to design a survey and gather quantitative information about the subject matter.

Qualitative | Interviews

To dive deeper into topic and look for opportunities we interviewed individuals, families and couples ranging from 15 to 45 years of age. The goal was to learn about their experiences and identify hurdles in their daily life with In context of the topic.

Key Insights

  • Most of the times people learn from Family & Friends.
  • Terms used & Language of laws and regulations is difficult to understand.
  • Nobody wants to know until it happens to them.
  • Social media is just good for what Is latest and what is in.
  • Most of the public schools doesn’t cater the need to educate people about laws & regulations.
  • People are aware of social media and how it plays an important role.
  • Its not just about laws and regulations it is also about cultural constraints.
  • Concept of state laws can be different in different state.
  • Context, Time and willingness to read and have information about laws is a big barrier.

Designing Personas

When we have data from secondary research, interviews and surveys we mapped these data points on likes/dislikes, user need, behaviors and motivations and designed personas based on that.

Personas

“I usually don’t go to websites just to learn about laws and regulations until something happens.” - Participant

“Luckily, A stranger came to me and said. 'Just in case you don't know, I saw a guy getting a ticket just because he was flying drone here.'” - Participant

“I had a really bad experience there [unknown city], I wish I could avoid unsafe spots.” - Participant

“I never knew you can not ride bicycles through [Savannah] squares.” - Participant

Affinity Diagramming

After defining our users we converted our observations to insights and start affinitizing and ideation process.

Design Question Re-framed

How might we facilitate sharing of information in community to help educate people about their surroundings?

Ideation

To have good idea, you need to have a lot of ideas. Based on the affinitized clusters we start generating as much as ideas we can.

Paper Prototyping & Testing

We had around nine ideas and we short listed based on the user needs and prototyping scope and selected 3 ideas to test with paper prototyping. Later, we looked at all our ideas and decided on final app idea incorporating different features from rest of ideas.

UX Testing

After having a working prototype, we invited users to test the app. At the end of session users were asked to fill out the assessment form based on their experience with the app. Overall, we got constructive feedback that helped us iterating on the designs two more times.
